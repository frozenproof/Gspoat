Silas Northwood

About the Author

Silas Northwood is not an author in the traditional sense. He does not invent his stories; he discovers them. Following a childhood fever that nearly claimed him, Silas was left with a unique and bewildering gift: the ability to lucidly navigate the vast, shared subconscious he calls "the Penumbra."

Every night, he walks the shorelines of forgotten memories, explores cities built from pure emotion, and speaks with the echoes of what was and what could be. His books are not works of fiction, but travelogues—field notes from a world that borders our own, a place all of us visit but none remember. He writes to record what he sees, and perhaps, to prove he is not mad.

Published Works

  • Whispers from the Ivory Shore

    Northwood's debut work, chronicling his first voyages into the Penumbra. It details the laws of this fluid reality and his encounters with the silent entities that tend to its shores.

  • The City of Perpetual Twilight

    A journey into a metropolis forged from nostalgia and regret. Here, the buildings rearrange themselves according to the mood of their phantom inhabitants, and the sun has not been seen in a thousand years.

  • A Map of Waking Thoughts

    Considered his most dangerous expedition, this book charts the turbulent wilderness between waking and sleeping, a chaotic land where concepts take physical form and half-formed ideas hunt travelers.

"People think dreams are fragile, fleeting things. They are not. They are the oldest places in the universe. We are the fleeting things that wander through them for a time." – Silas Northwood
